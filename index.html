Climate Change Awareness

Climate change is a worldwide phenomenon that is becoming increasingly dangerous. It is the shift in the global temperature, gradually over a sustained period of time.

Amazon Forest Fires

There have been more than 74,000 fires so far this year, the most ever recorded by the country’s National Institute for Space Research (INPE).

Greenland Icesheets

By the end of the summer, more than 400 billion metric tons of ice will have melted or broken off from Greenland’s huge ice sheet, scientists estimate.

Great Pacific Garbadge Patch

The Great Pacific Garbage Patch, also known as the Pacific trash vortex, spans waters from the West Coast of North America to Japan.

Impacts

Because of climate change, our rainfall patterns are becoming unpredictable, temperatures are soaring, natural disasters are getting deadlier. Impacts of climate change include:
  • The increasing length growing season
  • The changing average precipitation
  • The inceasing frequency of drought and heatwave
  • The increasing frequency of hurricanes with more strength
  • Rising sea levels and the melting of the arctic

Potential Solutions

Solutions to climate change will require we, as humans, to change the way that we behave, and to shift the way that we make and consume energy. Although we may make individual changes such as recycling and conserving water, this will not be enough to make as much of a difference in the long term. Most changes must come from industry and the government. There are many national and global steps that can be taken towards solving the climate change issue. Potential changes include:
  • Quickly replacing fossil fuels with cleaner renewable energy such as wind and solar power
  • Prohibiting the destruction of forests for intensive agricuture like cattle farming and palm oil plantation.
  • Creating ocean sanctuaries
  • Protecting native land
