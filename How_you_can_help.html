Improve your fitness and use public transport

Petrol and diesel cars release a lot of climate-wreckin emissions. They also increase air pollution.

Help your fitness and tackle climate change at the same time by cycling or walking. Too tired? Hop on the bus or train.

Get your electricity from the wind and the sun.

When you switch to a green-energy provider, you will be helping the movement for more renewable sources.

You could even be in for a surprise saving. Some green-energy provides to look at including but is not limited to:

  • British gas

Eat less meat

We are not saying go vegan or anything but eating less meat will help the amount of emissions being emitted.

Meat and dairy production is responsible for 14.% of global climate-changing emissions. Too much meat is no good for your health either. Each year we could prevent thousands of early deaths in the UK, and save the NHS a fortune, by eating low-meat or no-meat diets.
